The Eternal Return of the Identical

This is an idea from Friedrich Nietzsche's philosophy, were he tells you to imagine that your life, exactly as it is, has been repeating eternally and will continue to do so eternally.

In other words, this life, your life, is not lived once, but forever and ever, the exact same.

This idea is extremely useful, because if you really focus on believing it and letting your feelings loose, most likely you'll think it's a cruel fate, to relive the same painful life, the same suffering, why?, why would I want to do that?, you might ask.

But here's the thing, we should strive to change that way of looking at life, we should strive to one day be able to look back and say that it was worth it, and that living this same life again and again is desirable.

It's not easy to understand why, but to put it in simple terms, good and evil, value and nothingness, are purely emotional concepts; those feelings that make you want to end your stay in this world and, let's say, go to the afterlife, or maybe simply die, or even better, to improve your living conditions and have a 'dign life', stem from the ugliest and weakest parts of the human condition.

When asked if you'd think the Eternal Return of the Identical to be a curse or a blessing, if your answer is "a curse", you're destroying the value of the world, your world is rotten and decaying. Oh, but if there was someone that could say it is a blessing, it surely would not be one of us mere mortals, one of us mere humans, how could we ever love life unconditionally?, how could we ever dare face destiny with no gravity?, if anyone were ever capable, they'd be beyond humanity, he would be called the Übermensch.


It is an undeniable fact that both woe and joy exist in this world.

It is not Good and Evil, it is Woe and Joy the most important things for us, for my kind of human.

And it is not a fight, like amongst Good and Evil, but a dance, which happens between this Woe and Joy of ours.

A complementary dance, a necessary dance, inside this jar that's known as the world, that has it's sugary things, and also has dirt and shit, all mixed together.

It is necessary, we say, for Woe and Joy to exist, and both are a blessing of this world, for we are can feel them, and we are humans, why would we deny our nature?, we are humans, we are men, and we suffer, that suffering is part of us.

And amongst the Woe and the Joy of the world, many men have been lost, and have gone to think the world is only woe.

They are mistaken. It is a fatal duty, for us, the sons of the ancient men who invented God, to recognize the existence of Joy, and to fight for it, so that one day we might say that we need not an afterlife, that we need only OUR life, THIS life, and were it to Return Eternally to us, Identically as it is, we'd happily laugh, and as Zarathustra taught us, we'd say to death "this was life?, once more!, once more!"


Truly, it is wisdom to say that, just as much as the deniars of the world insult it and escape it, with an even greater force we can bless and enjoy it.

They've got the force to turn this world to nothing, to advance a nihilism that currupts it all. Ah, in our fate there's gotta be a path towards the victory of the earth, towards a victory of life over all death.
