Piracy is not real, because you can't steal an idea

When you "pirate" something on the internet, you're not pirating because you're not doing anything violent, and you're not stealing because your making a copy of something, which does not render it useless to the "owner" of it.

If I steal a car, the owner of the car loses it, and I gain it; if I copy a movie or a game, I gain access to it, but the owner doesn't lose it.

Digital media is fundamentally different to physical property, it cannot be stolen, it can be copied without authorization instead.

Users copying a programs with no authorization from the owning company instead of buying it as requested does not harm the company's revenue, because if the users hadn't had the option to make the free copy of the program, they would not have opted to pay for it instead; they only got it because it was free of cost.

Solidarity is first and foremost for the quality of software, always give your good warez to whomever wants them, that's how the hundreds of thousands of libre software packages we've got today came to be.

When software is libre, it means that you can see the source code and alter it to change the behaviour of the program however you want, and importantly, it also means that whomever you distribute your modified software to can also have access to the source code and can also alter it and redistribute it however they want. This way, a company cannot use libre software unless they make their own product libre as well, or at least this is the case with the GNU license, even though there are modifications that allow the mere usage of libre software libraries alongside with proprietary software.

Libre software is inherently better than proprietary software, and that is because the latter has an economic incentive behind, which may or may not align with the wellbeing of the user of the software, which in our recent times of greed seldom align with the wellbeing of others; the former, libre software, is made primarily with the intention of being useful to the user.

Now, there's companies behind 'open source' projects, which aren't the same as libre projects, and their intentions are that of shilling their product until they create a monopoly, and once a bunch of people are on the bag, they close it, like recently with systemd becoming complicit in the global push for deanonimization and tyranny, after systemd itself has been viciously pushed and shilled into every distribution they could, with the intention, of course, of monopolizing.

If you want to destroy an idea, debate it, because cutting someone's tongue is an admission of defeat to their word.
