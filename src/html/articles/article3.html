Modern medicine is all about prolonging suffering

In our days, medicine does not cure, it treats.

Patients with very unfortunate illnesses will have to take pills and recieve treatment for the rest of their lives, whether they're short or long, they'll be permanently taken care of in order to survive.

Were we still in the past, before technology got to this point, and all those people would most certainly be dead, nobody would have been able to save them.

And this doesn't just go for complex and life long sickness, it also goes to things that are very much curable.

For example, from working too much with devices one can get migraines, obviously geting away from the stress, both physical and mental, would cure a person, but they prefer to take migraine pills and other kinds of pain-killers.

There's many conditions that are very stupid also, like obesity, or the myriad of mental illnesses and quirky things people have come up with. In the past, they would either change their ways or die (rarely), nowadays they get treated indefinitely, suffering all the way through, without fixing anything, but still being mesmerized by the doctor's authority.

And it doesn't help that the economic incentive resides mostly on treating, not curing, because the cure you only sell once, the treatment, thousands.
